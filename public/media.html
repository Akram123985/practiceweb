What is Media Literacy ?

Media literacy is the ability to analyze, evaluate, and create digital content responsibly.

How to Identify Fake News & Misinformation?

  1. Check the Source – Is it from a reputable news outlet (BBC, CNN, The Guardian)?

  2. Look at the Date – Old articles are sometimes reshared out of context.

  3. Cross-Check with Other Sources – If a story appears on multiple trusted websites, it's more likely to be true.

  4. Watch for Emotional Language – If the article tries to make you angry or scared, it might be misleading.

Common Types of Misinformation

  • Clickbait – Sensational headlines designed to attract clicks.

  • Deepfakes – AI-generated images or videos that look real.

  • Propaganda – Biased or misleading information spread to influence opinions.

Responsible Social Media Usage

  • Think Before You Share – Verify facts before spreading news.

  • Adjust Privacy Settings – Limit who can see your posts.

  • Engage in Positive Digital Interactions – Avoid spreading hate speech or cyberbullying.
